## What This Book Is

A popular science book about the exclusion zone — a confirmed phenomenon where water near hydrophilic surfaces behaves differently from bulk water. The book traces the science from its discovery in a laboratory in Seattle in 2003, through a contested theoretical debate, into the natural world (trees, joints, cells), and then into the wellness industry that built a billion-dollar empire on a misreading of the science.

The book is not a wellness book. It is not a takedown of Pollack. It is an honest account of a real phenomenon, a legitimate scientific argument, and the grift that grew up around both.

## Key Science — What Is Confirmed

- **Exclusion zone exists** — confirmed by 11+ independent research groups
- **EZ properties confirmed:** negative charge, slightly higher viscosity, optical distinctness, exclusion of particles and dissolved substances
- **Responds to infrared light** — grows up to 3x resting width after 5 min exposure
- **Present in living plant xylem** — Wang and Pollack 2024, Scientific Reports (NOTE: Pollack lab study, not independent)
- **Engineering applications confirmed** — filtration, biofouling, microfluidics (Elton et al. 2020)
- **Hydration lubrication in joints** — confirmed, Jacob Klein, Weizmann Institute, Wolf Prize
- **Protein hydration shells** — confirmed, affect protein dynamics and catalytic efficiency
- **DNA spine of hydration** — confirmed, structurally characterized
- **Biointerfacial water in living cells** — Nature Communications 2024, 1.4 molar concentration
- **Biomolecular condensates managing water** — Nature 2023, Imperial College London and MRC LMB

## Key Science — What Is NOT Confirmed

- H3O2 formula — never in peer reviewed publication with crystallographic support
- Hexagonal sheet structure — not confirmed by crystallographic measurement
- "Fourth phase" designation — contested; may overstate the case
- Health implications of EZ water — no clinical evidence whatsoever
- EZ water in bottles or vortex devices — impossible; EZ exists only at surfaces in real time
