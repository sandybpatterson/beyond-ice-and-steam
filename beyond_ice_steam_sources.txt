CHAPTER 3 — Eleven Labs Can't Be Wrong

SOURCE 7 — Primary critical review paper (KEY SOURCE FOR CHAPTERS 3-6)
Elton, D.C., Spencer, P.D., Riches, J.D., and Williams, E.D. (2020).
"Exclusion Zone Phenomena in Water — A Critical Review of Experimental
Findings and Theories."
International Journal of Molecular Sciences, 21(14), 5041.
Published: July 17, 2020.
Full text: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7404113/
ArXiv preprint: https://arxiv.org/abs/1909.06822
STATUS: Confirmed. This paper confirms:
  - EZ independently demonstrated by multiple groups (refs 1-11 = 11 groups)
  - Birefringence, neutron radiography, NMR experimental confirmations
  - Schurr diffusiophoresis theory as compelling alternative
  - Florea et al. confirmation of Schurr's growth predictions
  - Problems with Pollack's proposed hexagonal structure
  - EZ reaches hundreds of microns in width

SOURCE 8 — Plant xylem study
Wang, A. and Pollack, G.H. (2024).
"Exclusion-zone water inside and outside of plant xylem vessels."
Scientific Reports, 14, Article 12071. Published: May 27, 2024.
Full text: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-024-62983-3
PubMed: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/38802675/
PMC: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11130298/
STATUS: Confirmed. Specific confirmed figures:
  - Pumpkin vessels: EZ up to 240 ± 56 μm
  - Napa cabbage, celery, asparagus: 133 ± 22 to 142 ± 20 μm
  - EZ builds from inside edge of vessel toward center
  - Infrared energy identified as efficient EZ builder
  - Limitations noted: cut-out vessel samples not intact living plants
  *** ATTRIBUTION NOTE — SEE CORRECTIONS SECTION ***

CHAPTER 4 — The Excluded Zone

SOURCE 11 — EZ size reaching hundreds of micrometers
Conventional expectation: 1-2 nanometers (few water molecule layers).
Actual EZ: hundreds of micrometers.
STATUS: Confirmed via Elton et al. (2020) and Pollack lab research page.
URL: https://www.pollacklab.org/research

SOURCE 12 — Charge separation in EZ
NMR, infrared, birefringence, electrical potential, and viscosity measurements
collectively show EZ is physically distinct ordered phase.
STATUS: Confirmed via Pollack lab research page and Elton et al. (2020).

SOURCE 13 — Infrared light growing the EZ
Infrared energy identified as efficient builder of EZ.
Wang and Pollack (2024) confirms. Also in Pollack (2013).
"Up to three times resting width after five minutes" — from Pollack lab.
STATUS: Partially confirmed. Three-times figure needs direct peer reviewed
citation. FLAG FOR CITATION PASS.

SOURCE 14 — Viscosity of EZ
EZ shows slightly higher viscosity than bulk water. Measured via falling
ball viscometry showing gel-like consistency.
STATUS: Confirmed in secondary sources. Direct peer reviewed citation
needed beyond Pollack lab sources. FLAG FOR CITATION PASS.

CHAPTER 5 — Two Theories Walk Into a Lab

SOURCE 15 — Daniel Elton confirmation and diffusiophoresis argument
Elton, D.C. et al. (2020) — same as Source 7.
Confirmed EZ phenomenon while arguing Pollack's structural explanation
is wrong. Supports Schurr's diffusiophoresis theory.
STATUS: Confirmed.

SOURCE 16 — Problems with hexagonal structure and flow
Elton et al. (2020) notes several problems with Pollack's proposed structure.
A structure rigid enough to behave as described should not flow.
STATUS: Confirmed.

SOURCE 17 — Polywater comparison
Elton et al. (2020) explicitly draws comparison to polywater, noting
problems with Pollack's structure were noted in the 1960s when a similar
structure was proposed for polywater.
STATUS: Confirmed.

CORRECTION 3 — CHAPTERS 1 AND 2 (SIGNIFICANT)
"The physics of suction only works up to about thirty feet" is
partially misleading.
Facts:
  - Simple atmospheric suction limit: approximately 10 meters / 33 feet.
    This part is accurate.
  - Mainstream explanation for tall trees is Cohesion-Tension (CT) theory
    — transpiration pull, cohesion between water molecules, negative
    pressure. This can theoretically reach over 400 feet.
  - Tallest known redwood: 379 feet.
  - CT theory is established and does not require exclusion zone.
ACTION: Revise Chapters 1 and 2 to acknowledge CT theory as the
mainstream explanation. Frame exclusion zone as a possible additional
mechanism being explored, not as filling an unexplained gap. Chapter 8
(The Engine in the Tree) must handle this carefully and fully.

CORRECTION 4 — CHAPTERS 1 AND 4 (SIGNIFICANT)
Joint lubrication claim presented as if confirmed by exclusion zone
research. This is Pollack's hypothesis — not independently confirmed
by clinical or orthopedic research.
ACTION: Reframe in both chapters as "a proposed explanation" rather
than confirmed fact. Add qualifier: "Pollack's lab has proposed..."
