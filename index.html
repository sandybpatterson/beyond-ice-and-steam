Beyond Ice and Steam

Water's Secret Life

A nonfiction book in 16 chapters

A popular science book about the exclusion zone — a confirmed phenomenon where water near hydrophilic surfaces behaves differently from bulk water. The book traces the science from its discovery in a laboratory in Seattle in 2003, through a contested theoretical debate, into the natural world (trees, joints, cells), and then into the wellness industry that built a billion-dollar empire on a misreading of the science.

This is not a wellness book. It is not a takedown of Pollack. It is an honest account of a real phenomenon, a legitimate scientific argument, and the grift that grew up around both.
